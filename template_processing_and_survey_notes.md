### **After Action Report**

This section is a one-pager that summarizes what was processed, what
actions were taken, and next steps for the collection. It should be
first in the report, but will be filled out last (after processing is
complete).

- *Summary of materials processed*

- *Summary of what has not been processed (if anything) both digital and
  analog*

- *Major challenges encountered and their resolution / lessons learned*

- *Links to:*

  - *Digital objects in the catalog (Searchworks)*

  - *Updated catalog record & finding aid*

  - *Blog post about processing or other external publicity.*

### **Survey Notes**

This section is an overview of born-digital materials in the collection.
It should be completed first as it informs the processing plan.

- *Collection Overview*

  - *Date(s) accessioned*

  - *Extent of collection - digital and analog*

  - *Location of digital files on the born-digital preservation lab
    server*

- *Born Digital Overview*

  - *Number of computer media (if applicable)*

  - *Total file size of born digital component of collection*

  - *Any other relevant information about files*

  - *Summary and link to any technical metadata reports (e.g.
    [<u>Brunnhilde</u>](https://github.com/tw4l/brunnhilde))*

### **Processing Plan**

This section is a proposed processing plan for the collection. It should
be completed before processing begins and submitted to a supervisor for
approval. A processing plan can be highly iterative and can tackle one
section of the collection at a time, informed by technical restrictions
or the research interest of groups of files.

- *Proposal for processing*

  - *What files will and will not be processed - rationale for not
    processing files if applicable*

### **Processing Notes**

This section is a log of actions taken and decisions made while
processing the collection. It is useful for the archivist to keep track
of their progress and, given the highly iterative nature of born-digital
processing, is also a useful log to avoid duplicative work for anyone
picking up the collection in the future.

- *Daily log of processing actions*

  - *Date*

  - *Hours worked*

  - *Bullet points of what was accomplished*

  - *Description of issues encountered*
