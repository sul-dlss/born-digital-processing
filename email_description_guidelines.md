## **Email Description Guidelines**

This document provides guidelines and in some cases, specific language
to use for describing email collections in archival finding aids,
catalog records, and the ePADD email processing and access software at
Stanford Libraries in the Department of Special Collections and
University Archives. It also offers recommendations for how much detail
to provide as minimal and enhanced description.

## **Physical Description**

*ArchivesSpace: Extent &gt; Physical Details*

*EAD: Extent &lt;extent&gt;*

*MARC: 300*

*ePADD: n/a*

This descriptive element does not always apply to email collections. In
the case that an email collection is transferred to the archive via
email, file transfer, or IMAP, then there will be no physical carrier to
document.

## **Extent**

*ArchivesSpace: Extent*

*EAD: Extent &lt;extent&gt;*

*MARC: 300*

*ePADD: Extent*

<span class="mark">For finding aids and catalog records, the extent
field should represent the extent of the collection as a whole. The
ePADD metadata displayed in the Delivery and Discovery Modules will
contain the extent for the email specifically, but this information is
auto populated by ePADD and no action from the processor is needed to
describe it.</span>

<u>Example(s):</u>

*From the [<u>Ted Nelson email
archive</u>](https://epadd.stanford.edu/epadd/collection-detail?collection=ePADD%20archive%20of%20Ted%20Nelson-Discovery)
(below is how the extent displays in ePADD):*

Messages:229,071

> Attachments:34,897
>
> Images:21,915
>
> Documents:3,929
>
> Others:9,053

## **Conditions Governing Access**

*ArchivesSpace: Conditions Governing Access Note*

*EAD: Conditions Governing Access &lt;accessrestrict&gt;*

*MARC: 506*

*ePADD: Notes*

This field should be used to record information about any messages that
are restricted, including a general reason for the restriction, the
duration of the restriction and the date at which all messages will be
made available. Note whether any messages are restricted from the ePADD
Discovery Module but are available in the reading room. Also include
information about how to access the full text version of the email
collection.

<u>Example(s):</u>

*From the [<u>John Perry Barlow
papers</u>](https://searchworks.stanford.edu/view/13312451):*

> The full text version of the email contained in this collection is
> available in the Field Reading Room; a redacted version, displaying
> correspondents and extracted entities (personal and corporate names
> and locations) from Barlow's email have been published in Stanford's
> online discovery module:
> [<u>http://epadd.stanford.edu/epadd/collections</u>](http://epadd.stanford.edu/epadd/collections).
> 452 messages have been entirely restricted from both the discovery
> module and the full text version available in the reading room
> according to federal and state guidelines, and Stanford Libraries
> policy, for up to 80 years. These messages may contain financial,
> medical, legal, and other sensitive information. They will be made
> available in 2089. Additionally, due to the potentially sensitive
> nature of their content, 217 messages are available only in the
> full-text version of the collection that is accessible in the Field
> Reading Room at Green Library and have been restricted from the
> redacted version that is available in the discovery module.

## **Scope and Content**

*ArchivesSpace: Scope and Content Note*

*EAD: Scope and Content &lt;scopecontent&gt;*

*MARC: 520*

*ePADD: Scope and Contents*

The Scope and Content note can be used at multiple levels within a
finding aid, including collection, series, subseries, or item. Due to
this, there may be some repetition of information within a finding aid,
but try to use a level of specificity or detail that is appropriate to
the level of description i.e. a series level scope and content note for
the email series would be more detailed than a collection level scope
and content note that describes the entire collection.

<u>Example(s):</u>

<u>Collection Level:</u>

*From the [<u>John Perry Barlow
papers</u>](https://oac.cdlib.org/findaid/ark:/13030/c8zs344q/):*

> John Perry Barlow's papers cover the entirety of his life, although
> there is far more physical material about his earlier years, and his
> later years are probably better represented in digital files (which
> are partially closed until they are fully processed). Correspondence
> has been separated into personal and professional categories and then
> sorted chronologically, with selected letters refiled in other more
> relevant series (such as all music publishing correspondence placed in
> the Grateful Dead series.) **The collection also contains Barlow's
> email account dating from 1996-2009. The email contains correspondence
> related to Barlow's life, work with the Electronic Frontier
> Foundation, and personal and family relationships. The email also
> documents Barlow's speaking engagements for various academic
> institutions and his prolific world travels.** There are also series
> for Barlow's writing, as well as writing about him. Most
> technology/cyberspace files can be found under Electronic Frontier
> Foundation and the more generic Technology & Computers series. Some
> categories such as the Photographs and Media series include material
> from a variety of sources and subjects.

<u>Series Level</u>:

*From the [<u>Helen and Newton Harrison
papers</u>](https://oac.cdlib.org/findaid/ark:/13030/c89g5twm/):*

> Email in accessions 2016-053 and 2016-054 were captured from the
> Harrison Studio Gmail and AOL email accounts with ePADD software
> resulting in over 77,000 messages. This sub-series is comprised of:
> 68,110 incoming messages and 9,636 outgoing; 25,145 attachments (7,161
> images, 5,466 documents, and 12,518 other); 15,170 correspondents;
> extracted entities (114,865 persons, 124,823 organizations, and 69,780
> locations).
>
> <u>ePADD Delivery & Discovery Module:</u>
>
> *From the [<u>Ted Nelson
> papers</u>](https://epadd.stanford.edu/epadd/collection-detail?collection=ePADD%20archive%20of%20Ted%20Nelson-Discovery):*
>
> The Ted Nelson email archive spans the years of 2001 to 2019. The
> archive contains correspondence related to Ted Nelson’s life and
> career as an information technology entrepreneur and philosopher. Many
> of Nelson’s projects are documented through this email collection,
> including Project Xanadu, XanaduSpace, ZigZag, and his 2010
> autobiography, Possiplex: Movies, Intellect, Creative Control, My
> Computer Life and the Fight for Civilization. The correspondence also
> documents Nelson’s speaking and teaching engagements at the University
> of California, Santa Cruz and Chapman University. Other well-known
> information technology figures found among Ted Nelson’s correspondence
> include Douglas Engelbart, Steve Wozniak, Harry Mendell, and Laurie
> Spiegel.

## **Processing Information**

*ArchivesSpace: Processing Information Note*

*EAD: Processing Information &lt;processinfo&gt;*

*MARC: 583*

*ePADD: Notes*

This field can be used to describe the steps taken and tools used to
process the email

portion of a collection.

<u>Example(s):</u>

*From the [<u>Donald E. Knuth
papers</u>](https://oac.cdlib.org/findaid/ark:/13030/kt2k4035s1/dsc/?query=don%20knuth%20papers#aspace_18dab79b05c98be59b353d42695cac61):*

> Messages were processed with ePADD software
> (https://library.stanford.edu/projects/epadd). It was screened for
> messages containing personal identifying information (PII) and
> sensitive content and correspondents were edited. The correspondents
> and extracted entities (personal and corporate names and locations)
> have been published in Stanford's online discovery module:
> [<u>http://epadd.stanford.edu/epadd/collections</u>](http://epadd.stanford.edu/epadd/collections).
>
> This email collection was processed in two parts due to significant
> technical differences between the email formats Knuth used.
> Researchers will encounter duplicate messages across both parts. Note
> that email in RMAIL format (dating from January 1999 through December
> 2018) consists only of sent messages, while email in MBOX format
> (November 2010 through January 2019) consists of both sent and
> received messages.

## **Topical Subject Headings**

*ArchivesSpace: Agent &gt; Subject*

*EAD: Subject &lt;subject&gt;*

*MARC: 650*

*ePADD: n/a*

Topical subject headings should be used to capture the high level topics
represented in the email collection. These may overlap with the subject
headings already in use for the collection.

<u>Example(s):</u>

*From the* [*<u>Ted Nelson
papers</u>*](https://searchworks.stanford.edu/view/13135739) *(below is
exact format to be used in marc records):*

650 0 Computer science x| History.

## **Person Added Entries**

*ArchivesSpace: Agent &gt; Subject &gt; Role = Creator*

*EAD: Personal Name &lt;persname&gt;*

*MARC: 700\_1*

*ePADD: n/a*

Person added entries should be used to record the names of
correspondents present in the collection. For a more minimal
description, only include the names within the top 10 correspondents
that also have LCNAF authorities.

> <u>Example(s):</u>
>
> *From the [<u>Helen and Newton Harrison
> papers</u>](https://searchworks.stanford.edu/view/9068354) (below is
> the exact format to be used in marc records):*
>
> 700 1 Haley,David,e|correspondent
>
> 700 1 Fox,William L.,d|1949-e|correspondent
