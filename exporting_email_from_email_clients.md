# **Exporting Email from Email Clients**

This document provides step by step directions for exporting email from
various email clients.

It is important to note that the option for a user to export their own
email from within the client may not be available for all accounts
depending on whether they are managed by an organization like an
employer. If that’s the case, they will need to contact their IT staff
for help.

## Exporting only messages with certain labels

### Creating and applying labels

If you only wish to export a certain subset of messages, the easiest way
to do so is by applying labels to your messages. Google’s documentation
on how to create, edit, and delete labels is here:
[<u>https://support.google.com/mail/answer/118708</u>](https://support.google.com/mail/answer/118708?hl=en&co=GENIE.Platform%3DDesktop#zippy=%2Clabel-messages-in-your-inbox%2Clabel-a-message-youre-writing%2Cmove-a-message-to-another-label%2Cedit-a-label)

You can apply labels to individual messages or to a group of messages.
To apply labels to one or more messages, you can check or uncheck the
checkboxes to the left of the messages in Gmail’s inbox view. In the
below screenshot, the top two messages are selected. You can apply a
label to those messages by clicking on the icon in the red box.

<img src="/Users/debauche/Downloads/images/media/image6.png"
style="width:4.66146in;height:1.42682in" />

You can also apply labels to the results of a search in your Gmail
account. To do so, enter your search terms in the bar at the top of your
inbox. (Google’s documentation on search operators you can use is here:
[<u>https://support.google.com/mail/answer/7190</u>](https://support.google.com/mail/answer/7190).)
Then, click the “select all” checkbox in the top left corner of the
search results, in the yellow box below. To select all search results
and not just the ones on the current page, click the text at the top of
the search results that says “Select all \## conversations in \[your
search term\]” (blue box). You can then apply a label (red box).

## Exporting Email Accounts from MS Outlook or Hotmail

**Note on Hotmail:** Hotmail is a legacy email client but can be
accessed through the Microsoft Outlook email client.

**Windows Desktop Client:**

1.  Open email account in Windows Microsoft Outlook desktop application

2.  In the **File** menu, select **Open/Export**.

3.  A dialog box will appear. Select **Export to a File**, then click
    Next, and select Outlook Data File in the following box.

4.  A dialog box will appear. Select **Export to a File**, then click
    Next, and select **Outlook Data File** in the following box.

5.  Now you will see the folders in your email account. Select the
    folder that you created earlier and click Next.

6.  Choose the destination where you want to save the folder and click
    Finish. Your emails will be saved in your chosen directory.

**Web Client:**

1.  Open email account in Windows Microsoft Outlook web based client

2.  Select the “**Settings Gear**” in the top right corner of the screen

3.  Go to **Privacy and Data**

4.  Select **Export Mailbox**
