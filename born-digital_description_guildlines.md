## **Born Digital Description Guidelines**


This document provides guidelines and in some cases, specific language
to use for describing born digital collections in archival finding aids
and catalog records. It also offers recommendations for how much detail
to provide as minimal and enhanced description.

**Description of Element**

This element provides a word or phrase by which the material being
described is known or can be identified. A title may be devised or
formal. Titles can occur at the Collection, Series, and Item level. In
the case that there is no formal title available, the processor should
devise a title. Devised titles typically reflect the content or format
of the materials being described.

Processors should note that collection level titles are typically
assigned by the Accessioning Archivist.

Series level titles often reflect the format of the material being
described, so “Born Digital” is a typical series level title.

**Example(s)**

<u>Series Level:</u>

Born Digital & Hardware *(From the [<u>John Perry Barlow
papers</u>](https://oac.cdlib.org/findaid/ark:/13030/c8zs344q/))*

<u>Item Level:</u>

Digital files 1988-2022 *(From the [<u>Leon Sun
papers</u>](https://oac.cdlib.org/findaid/ark:/13030/c80008q7/))*

> Plus tard tu comprendras screenplay, draft (structure dialogue -
> commentaires HG.pdf) *(From the [<u>Amos Gitai film
> archive</u>](https://searchworks.stanford.edu/view/gn993tr5328))*

**Description of Element**

Dates can occur at the Collection, Series, and Item level. Note whether
a date or date range is the date created or date last modified if known.
Dates can be an inclusive range or a single date.

**Example(s)**

<u>Inclusive date range:</u>

circa 1990-2013 *(From the [<u>Helen and Newton Harrison
papers</u>](https://oac.cdlib.org/findaid/ark:/13030/c89g5twm/))*

<u>Single date:</u>

2017-07-14 *(From the [<u>Atari Design
interviews</u>](https://searchworks.stanford.edu/view/zw806ny3463))*

<u>Date created:</u>

> Date created \[ca. January 10, 1990\] *(From the [<u>Unity/La Unidad
> Archive Project
> collection</u>](https://searchworks.stanford.edu/view/db978dt8028))*

**Example(s)  **

<u>An “inferred” date in MODS displays in brackets:</u>

\[June 18, 1970 - June 19, 1970\] *(From the [<u>Albert Joseph Guérard
papers</u>](https://searchworks.stanford.edu/view/bv351wx3024))*

<u>A “questionable” date in MODS displays in brackets, with a question
mark:</u>

\[1977?\] *(From the [<u>Albert Joseph Guérard
papers</u>](https://searchworks.stanford.edu/view/wp909jk3102))*

**Another strategy**

The “uncertainty” field is not explicit as to why the date is uncertain.
Adding a free-text note, as in the below MODS example, allows the
archivist to explain the technical source of the uncertainty.

&lt;note type="admin"&gt;Creation date is based on file modification
time and may not reflect the actual content creation date.&lt;/note&gt;
*(From the [<u>Elbert “Big Man” Howard
papers</u>](https://searchworks.stanford.edu/view/fg311cv8683))*

**Description of Element**

The physical and/or digital extent of the materials being described.
[<u>DACS
specifies</u>](https://saa-ts-dacs.github.io/dacs/06_part_I/03_chapter_02/05_extent.html)
extent should be expressed first as a quantity, then as a description of
the physical format.

**Example(s)**

<u>Unformatted:</u>

636.4 linear feet (6660 audiovisual elements, 66 feet of manuscripts,
230.3 gigabytes of born-digital files) *(From the [<u>New Dimensions
Media records</u>](https://searchworks.stanford.edu/view/6976115))*

<u>Highly formatted:</u>

a|0.69f|GB a|(3f|optical discs) *(From the [<u>Native American Arts
Alliance records</u>](https://searchworks.stanford.edu/view/5762796))*

a| Approximately 30 f| linear feet a| (61 boxes), a| 98.9 f| GB *(From
the [<u>Reese Erlich
papers</u>](https://searchworks.stanford.edu/view/13112691))*

<u>Repeated 300 field:</u>

For collections where digital materials have been delivered both on
computer media (e.g. hard drive) and via cloud/FTP, you may prefer to
represent the collection in gigabyte extent only, or to repeat the 300
field.

|3physical material|a1.0|flinear feet|a(3|fhard drives)

|3digital material|a693.1|fGB

## **Access Points**

**Description of Element**

This overarching category can include several elements with differing
titles depending on the metadata standard used. In general, these are
terms from controlled vocabularies that are used to aid user discovery
for a collection or digital object. If an official term, name, subject,
etc. is not present in the relevant controlled vocabulary, use a local
term.

**Description of Element**

that are closely related to the materials being described by provenance,
sphere of activity, or subject matter, either in the same repository, in
other repositories, or elsewhere.
