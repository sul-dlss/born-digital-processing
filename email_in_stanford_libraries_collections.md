## Email in Stanford Libraries collections

#### Why donate your email to Stanford Libraries?

Email is a rich source of historical documentation -- it’s the modern
form of correspondence, but it’s so much more! Email documents our work,
creative processes, and relationships with friends, family, and
professional colleagues. It also includes artifacts like photos,
documents, and links.

Stanford is a leader in the area of archiving email. We developed the
technology to curate email collections and we are one of the only
archives that regularly processes email collections and makes them
accessible for research.

#### What does Stanford Libraries do with email collections?

We use our email curating software,
[<u>ePADD</u>](https://library.stanford.edu/projects/epadd), to process
email collections and make them accessible to researchers in the Special
Collections reading room. All email collections go through a thorough
screening process where any legally protected, sensitive, or
confidential information is restricted from access. We welcome your
engagement in this process in any of the following ways:

- Providing a list of individuals or topics that should be restricted
  with time periods for those restrictions

- Meeting with curators or archivists to review your collection

- Reviewing your collection prior to transferring it to Stanford
  Libraries using ePADD (Stanford archivists will assist you with the
  process of setting up ePADD on your computer)

Finally, we publish metadata from processed email collections to our
[<u>online ePADD discovery
platform</u>](https://epadd.stanford.edu/epadd/collections) to help
researchers find our collections.

#### How to donate email?

If you have email files, please include them with the rest of the
digital material that you are transferring to Stanford Libraries.
Stanford digital archivists can also assist with exporting your email
from the email client that you use.
